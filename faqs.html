How do you charge?
+
We charge a transparent fee that we agree with you upfront. Depending on your situation this may be a flat advisory fee, an annual retainer or a percentage of assets under management. We do not earn any commissions, referral fees or trail income from any product. What you pay us is what we earn. Nothing else.
What is a fiduciary and why should I care?
+
A fiduciary is legally required to act in your interest. Not in their own interest. Not in their firm's interest. Yours. Most brokers and financial salespeople in the US operate under a suitability standard, which simply means what they recommend has to be broadly suitable for you. It does not mean it has to be the best option available. As a registered investment advisor, we are held to a fiduciary standard at all times. That is a meaningful and important difference.
Do I need a certain amount of money to work with you?
+
We do not have a strict minimum. What matters more is whether you are serious about your financial future and whether we can genuinely add value. The first call costs you nothing. If it turns out we are not the right fit, we will say so honestly and point you in a direction that works better for your situation.
How is this different from my bank's relationship manager?
+
Your broker or advisor at a large firm is employed by that firm. Their job is to serve the firm's interests while also being helpful to you. Those two things are often in conflict. They have products to sell, minimums to hit and managers watching their numbers. We have none of that. We only work for you, and the only way we stay in business is if you find enough value to keep paying us directly. That changes everything about the advice you receive.
Do you manage investments directly?
+
Yes. As a registered investment advisor, we can manage your portfolio directly. We build low-cost, diversified portfolios using index funds and ETFs. We do not use proprietary products or receive any compensation from fund companies. Your portfolio is built around your goals, your timeline and your risk tolerance.
How often will we meet?

At minimum, once a year for a full plan review. More often when your life changes. Job change, inheritance, new child, approaching retirement, selling a business. Any event that affects your finances is a reason to talk. You also have direct access to reach us between reviews if something comes up.
