Can you really be a "fiduciary" if you earn commissions?
+
Yes — they're separate things. "Commission" is how I'm paid; "best-interest standard" is the legal duty I owe you when recommending an annuity or life insurance product. Under [State]'s insurance best-interest standard, I'm required to put your interests ahead of my own and to be able to show my reasoning. Being independent of any single carrier is what makes that duty practical: I have no quota pushing me toward one company.
Does working with you cost more than going directly to a carrier?
+
No. Carrier compensation is built into product pricing industry-wide — whether you buy directly from the carrier, through a captive agent, or through an independent producer like me. Working with an independent agent does not increase your premium. You get the comparison and the ongoing service at no additional cost to you.
How exactly are you paid?
+
By commission, paid directly by the insurance carrier whose product you select — not by you, and not as a fee deducted from your premium or account value. Commission rates and structures vary by product and carrier, and I'll disclose them to you, on request, before any application is signed. The full breakdown is in the Compensation & Relationship Disclosure.
Which carriers do you represent?
+
I'm appointed with a panel of multiple carriers rather than employed by any one of them, which lets me compare actual policy terms instead of one company's lineup. My current carrier appointments and the products available in your state are shared during our conversation, since availability changes over time and varies by state.
Do you handle investments — stocks, mutual funds or a brokerage account?
+
No. I hold a life, health and annuity insurance license. I am not registered with FINRA or the SEC and I do not offer, sell or advise on securities such as stocks, bonds, mutual funds, variable annuities or variable life. If your situation calls for those, I'll tell you directly and point you to a properly registered professional.
What if I need something outside your license?
+
I'll say so, plainly. Where a strategy involves securities, investment advisory services, tax filing or legal documents, I'll refer you — where appropriate — to a FINRA-registered representative, an SEC- or state-registered investment adviser, a CPA or an attorney. I'd rather send you to the right person than stretch beyond what I'm licensed to do.
Do I need a certain amount of money to work with you?
+
No strict minimum. What matters is whether I can genuinely help your situation. The first conversation costs you nothing. If we're not the right fit, I'll say so honestly and point you somewhere that works better for you.
